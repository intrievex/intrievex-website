FAQ
Q: What services are available from Intrievex?
A: Intrievex provides clients with the ability to access court records via three different methods. Online (real-time access through CourtsOnline application), daily data feeds, and backfill (historical) data loads. Intrievex provides access to court records in Washington, Oregon, Idaho, and counties in Illinois, Minnesota, and Texas—with more states and counties coming into production. Available cases include State, County and Municipal courts along with U.S. bankruptcy cases.
Q: What types of information can I access using the online client application?
A: The CourtsOnline application allows access to a specific case or judgment by its number in any court or searching in any court(s) for all occurrences of a specific person or business name. Retrieved case details can be displayed, printed, saved to a file, or emailed to a colleague.
Q: Is statewide index searching available for Washington?
A: Yes. Index searches are available in the COL Application Version 4.xx (or newer). Index records are retrieved in blocks of up to 500 records per search. Numerous advanced search types are available including search by participants, county, and many more. Index searching is a quick way to locate court activity for a specific person or business when you don't know their location or the past history is old or unknown. You can search all 39 counties in Washington in about ten seconds and approximately 3,000 records can be returned in 60 seconds.
Q: What port number and transmission protocol must be enabled in our firewall to support the CourtsOnline client application?
A: The client application requires port number 85. The transmission protocol is TCP/IP, no UDP, and both in and out traffic must be enabled.
Q: What IP addresses must be enabled in our firewall to support the online client application?
A: The client application selects from a pool of IP addresses, as needed, to make a fast and reliable connection. Currently, the IP 216.168.61.131 and/or one from this range: 64.38.134.178 to 64.38.134.193 may be used. Note: Client version 4.xx (or newer) will automatically select the best IP to use for the current session and no additional settings are necessary by the user or IT personnel.
Q: How much does the client application cost?
A: The client application is provided at no cost to clients that have established an account with us. We only charge for access and retrieval transactions and make the application available via Internet download, email, U.S. Postal Service, or CD-ROM.

For additional pricing information, please call us at (425) 497-1900 to speak with one of our representatives. Typically you can establish an account and begin using our services in about 30 minutes or less.
Q: When are the services available?
A: The CourtsOnline service is available 24 hours a day, 365 days a year. The only time service may be unavailable is during periodic maintenance sessions by the courts. Typically, maintenance is done between 12:00 a.m. and 5:00 a.m. on Saturdays or Sundays.
Q: How do I email search results from the online application?
A: To email a report, simply activate a search results form by clicking anywhere on the form. Next, select File > Save As from the main menu, and save the report to a text file (.txt). The text file can then be emailed to your recipient.
Q: Can the client application be used on a Mac computer?
A: Yes. If Windows is installed on it.
